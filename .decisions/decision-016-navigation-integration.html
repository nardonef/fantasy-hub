Navigation Integration

Information Architecture

Your app currently has a 5-tab bar: Dashboard, Analytics, League, AI Chat (V2 placeholder), and Profile. You're adding three new views: Feed (social/news), Recommendations (waiver/start-sit signals), and Player Detail (aggregate intelligence). The question is where these live. Adding more tabs risks overcrowding the tab bar on smaller phones. Nesting them under existing tabs might hide them. This is a genuine IA tension -- the intelligence layer is big enough to deserve its own top-level presence, but you're already at 5 tabs.

The AI Chat tab is a V2 placeholder that isn't built yet. Replace it with an "Intel" tab that houses the intelligence platform. Feed is the root view, Recommendations and Player Detail are reached via push navigation within the tab. When AI Chat eventually ships in a later phase, it becomes a floating button or sheet accessible from any screen -- arguably a better UX than a dedicated tab anyway. This keeps you at 5 tabs total with no overcrowding. I recommend this because it gives Intelligence top-level visibility without adding tab bar clutter.

Works well when

  • Stays at 5 tabs -- no crowding on small screens

  • Intel gets prime tab real estate for maximum discoverability

Watch out for

  • AI Chat needs a new home later -- floating button or sheet pattern
  • Three views (Feed, Recs, Detail) in one tab could feel deep

Keep the AI Chat tab and add Intel as a 6th tab. iOS supports up to 5 visible tabs before auto-creating a "More" overflow, but many apps (Instagram, YouTube) successfully use 5 without issue. With 6, the last tab becomes "More" containing Profile -- or you shorten all labels to fit. This preserves the AI Chat tab's slot for when you build it, but makes the tab bar tighter. On iPhone SE/Mini screens, 6 tabs can feel cramped.

Works well when

  • Both Intel and AI Chat deserve permanent top-level access
  • Users on larger phones won't notice the tight spacing
  • No need to redesign when AI Chat ships later

Watch out for

  • 6 tabs triggers iOS "More" overflow on smaller screens
  • AI Chat tab would sit empty until that phase ships
  • Tab labels need to be very short to fit (3-5 chars)

Keep the existing 5 tabs untouched. Add Feed and Recommendations as sub-sections within the Dashboard tab, using a segmented control or horizontal scroll at the top to switch between "Overview" (existing dashboard), "Feed", and "Recs". Player Detail is accessed by tapping any player mention in any view. This is the least disruptive option -- the tab bar doesn't change at all, and intelligence data lives right next to existing dashboard analytics.

Works well when

  • Zero disruption to existing navigation structure
  • Dashboard becomes a "command center" with multiple perspectives
  • Easy to A/B test -- users can ignore the new sections

Watch out for

  • Feed and Recommendations are hidden behind a tab-within-a-tab
  • Dashboard already has a lot of content (Pulse, superlatives, sparkline)
  • Discoverability suffers -- new users may never find Intel sections

No dedicated Intel tab at all. Instead, distribute intelligence signals across existing views contextually. The Dashboard gets an "Intel Spotlight" card showing top signals for your roster. Analytics views get "social buzz" sections for relevant players. Manager profiles show recommendations for their team. Player Detail becomes a sheet accessible from any player mention anywhere. This is the most integrated approach -- intelligence is woven into the fabric of the app rather than siloed.

Works well when

  • Intelligence feels native, not bolted on -- it's everywhere contextually
  • No tab bar changes, no navigation redesign
  • Users encounter intel naturally while doing what they already do

Watch out for

  • No single place to browse all intelligence -- no "power user" destination
  • Much harder to build -- touching many existing views vs. one new tab
  • Filterable feed with source/team/player controls needs a home somewhere
