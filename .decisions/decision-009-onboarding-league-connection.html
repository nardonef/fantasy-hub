Decision 9 of 12

Onboarding & League Connection Flow

Interaction

The first 60 seconds of the app determine whether someone sticks around or deletes it. A new user needs to sign up, connect at least one fantasy league (which means OAuth with Yahoo, cookies with ESPN, or a league ID for Sleeper), wait for historical data to import, and then see something that makes them go "whoa, I didn't know that about my league." The challenge is that connecting a provider can be friction-heavy (especially Yahoo OAuth), and the initial data import takes time. How do you keep users engaged through that wait?

Progressive Reveal

1 Sign up (Apple Sign-In, 1 tap)
↓
2 Pick provider: Yahoo / ESPN / Sleeper
↓
3 OAuth / connect (provider-specific)
↓
4 Pick league(s) from discovered list
↓
5 Land on dashboard — stats appear as they import
↓
6 Quick wins animate in: record, rival, best week
Get the user to their dashboard as fast as possible, then progressively fill it in as data imports. In step 2, they pick a provider and connect. Step 4 shows all leagues found on their account — they pick which to import. Then immediately drop them on the dashboard (step 5). Current season data loads first (seconds), then historical seasons import in the background. As each season finishes importing, new stats and insights animate onto the dashboard — "You've won 3 championships," "Your longest win streak is 8 games," "Your biggest rival is Mike (14-9 all-time)." The progressive reveal turns the wait into a drip of dopamine hits. I recommend this because the user sees value immediately and each new insight keeps them hooked while the full import completes.

Works well when

  • User sees value within 30 seconds of connecting
  • Progressive reveals turn the wait into excitement
  • Current season loads fast — historical is bonus, not blocker

Watch out for

  • Dashboard needs to look good partially loaded (skeleton states)
  • Analytics that need full history show "importing..." temporarily

Option B

Import First, Wow After

1 Sign up + connect provider
↓
2 Pick league(s) to import
↓
3 Animated import screen with fun facts
↓
4 "Your league is ready" push notification
↓
5 Full dashboard — everything loaded at once
Make the user wait for the full import, but make the wait entertaining. Step 3 is a dedicated import screen with an animated progress bar, and as each season loads, fun facts pop up: "Importing 2019... found 247 transactions" or "2021 championship game: you won by 0.3 points!" When the import finishes (or if they leave and come back), they land on a fully loaded dashboard with all history — no partial states, no "importing..." badges. The push notification (step 4) re-engages users who left during import. The trade-off is a longer time to first value, but when they do see the dashboard it's the full experience.

Works well when

  • First impression is the complete experience — nothing half-loaded
  • Fun facts during import build anticipation
  • Push notification re-engages users who leave during import

Watch out for

  • Users may abandon during the wait (minutes for deep history)
  • Import screen needs to be genuinely entertaining
  • Higher drop-off risk than progressive reveal

Option C
Chosen

League Link / Invite First

1 Receive invite link from leaguemate
↓
2 See league preview (no sign-up yet)
↓
3 Sign up to claim your manager profile
↓
4 Connect provider to unlock your personal stats
↓
5 Full dashboard — league already imported
Flip the flow: instead of "sign up then connect," start with an invite link from someone who's already in the app. In step 1, a leaguemate shares a link. Step 2 shows a preview of the league — standings, some stats, the AI's take on the season — all without requiring sign-up. Then the user signs up (step 3) to claim their manager profile within that league, and optionally connects their provider (step 4) to unlock personal stats and other leagues. The league data is already imported because the person who invited them already connected it. This is the viral growth model — each user brings their whole league.

Works well when

  • Viral growth — one user imports league, invites 11 leaguemates
  • Zero wait for invited users — league is already imported
  • Preview before sign-up reduces friction

Watch out for

  • First user in a league still has the full import experience
  • Doesn't work for users who discover the app solo
  • Requires building the invite/preview infrastructure

Demo League First

1 Open app — instantly see demo league
↓
2 Explore analytics, chat with AI, browse stats
↓
3 "See your own league" prompt
↓
4 Sign up + connect provider
↓
5 Import runs — already know the app
Let users play with the app before they sign up. Step 1: open the app and immediately land in a pre-loaded demo league with 10 years of realistic history. They can explore analytics, try the AI chat ("who's the biggest bust in this league?"), browse head-to-head records — everything works. When they're sold on the product (step 3), they sign up and connect their real league. The import wait is less frustrating because they already understand and want the product. This is the "try before you buy" approach. The trade-off is building and maintaining a realistic demo dataset.

Works well when

  • Zero-friction first experience — no sign-up to see value
  • Users are already sold before they hit any friction
  • Great for App Store conversions — try instantly

Watch out for

  • Demo data needs to be realistic and maintained
  • Users may play with demo but never connect their own league
  • AI chat costs money even on demo data
