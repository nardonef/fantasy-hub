Phased Delivery Strategy

Interaction

You've made 11 decisions that define a very ambitious app. Now the question is: what ships first? The tension is between getting something into people's hands quickly (so you can validate the concept and get feedback) versus building enough that the app is genuinely impressive on first impression. Fantasy football is seasonal — if you ship mid-season, people might not bother until next year. If you wait for everything, you might never ship. This decision shapes the v1 feature set, what gets deferred to v2 and v3, and the overall timeline strategy.

Analytics-First Launch

V1 — CORE ANALYTICS

  • Yahoo + Sleeper provider sync
  • Progressive onboarding flow
  • Dashboard with league overview
  • All analytics categories (scrollable sections)
  • Head-to-head, standings, scoring trends
  • Draft grades, power rankings, records
  • Season filter (current / all-time)
  • League invite links
↓

V2 — AI + SOCIAL

  • AI chat with contextual triggers
  • ESPN provider adapter
  • Weekly recap narratives
  • Push notifications
  • Manager profile sharing
↓

V3 — ENGAGEMENT

  • Trade analyzer
  • Waiver recommendations
  • Playoff probability models
  • League awards ceremony
Ship the analytics engine first — that's the core value prop. V1 connects Yahoo and Sleeper (the two with the best APIs), imports all historical data, and gives users the full analytics dashboard: standings, head-to-head, scoring trends, draft grades, power rankings, and records. No AI chat yet — that's v2. The reasoning: analytics alone is a compelling product. Nobody else does multi-provider historical analytics well. The AI is exciting but it's also the riskiest, most expensive feature to get right. I recommend this because it lets you validate the core idea (do people actually want cross-platform analytics?) before investing heavily in AI. ESPN is deferred to v2 because their API is the most painful to integrate.

Works well when

  • Core value prop ships first — analytics is the hook
  • Validates demand before investing in AI
  • 2 providers is enough to prove cross-platform works

Watch out for

  • No AI in v1 — loses the "wow factor" differentiator
  • ESPN users are left out until v2

AI-First Vertical Slice

V1 — CHAT + BASIC STATS

  • Sleeper provider only (easiest API)
  • Minimal onboarding
  • Basic dashboard (standings, record)
  • AI chat — full conversational experience
  • Natural language stat lookups
  • Contextual triggers on basic views
↓

V2 — FULL ANALYTICS

  • Yahoo + ESPN providers
  • Complete analytics dashboard
  • Draft grades, trends, luck index
  • League invites + sharing
↓

V3 — ENGAGEMENT

  • Weekly AI narratives
  • Trade analyzer
  • Waiver recommendations
  • Awards + milestones
Lead with the AI — it's the most differentiated feature. V1 connects Sleeper only (public API, no OAuth headaches), imports league data, and ships the full AI chat experience with a basic stats dashboard. The bet: people will share the AI conversations ("look what this app knows about our league") and that drives organic growth. The analytics dashboard is intentionally minimal in v1 — just enough data views for the AI to reference. This is riskier because the AI needs to be genuinely good on day one, and you're limiting your addressable market to Sleeper users initially.

Works well when

  • Most shareable — AI conversations drive word-of-mouth
  • Sleeper-only makes v1 integration simple
  • Differentiator ships day one

Watch out for

  • AI must be great on day one — no room for mediocre
  • Sleeper-only limits initial addressable market
  • LLM costs from day one with no proven revenue

Everything-Lite MVP

V1 — EVERYTHING (LITE)

  • All 3 providers (Yahoo, ESPN, Sleeper)
  • Full onboarding flow
  • Dashboard + basic analytics (3 sections)
  • AI chat (text-only, no rich cards)
  • League invites
  • Basic head-to-head + standings
↓

V2 — DEPTH

  • Rich AI responses (charts, tables)
  • Full analytics (all categories)
  • Draft grades, luck index, trends
  • Contextual AI triggers
↓

V3 — POLISH

  • AI narratives + weekly recaps
  • Trade analyzer
  • Playoff models
  • Awards ceremony
Ship a little bit of everything. V1 includes all three providers, a basic analytics dashboard (standings, H2H, scoring trends — but not the full category scroll), text-only AI chat, and league invites. Nothing is deep, but nothing is missing either. The idea: users from any platform can join, they get a taste of analytics and AI, and you learn what they engage with most to decide where to go deeper in v2. The risk is that nothing is deep enough to be impressive — "jack of all trades, master of none" — and first impressions matter for retention.

Works well when

  • All platforms supported from day one — biggest addressable market
  • Users get a taste of every feature
  • Learn what users care about before going deep

Watch out for

  • Nothing is impressive enough to retain users
  • ESPN integration is painful and may delay launch
  • Text-only AI feels underwhelming after the marketing pitch

Big Bang Offseason Launch

V1 — FULL PRODUCT (OFFSEASON)

  • All 3 providers
  • Complete progressive onboarding
  • Full analytics dashboard (all sections)
  • AI chat with rich responses + triggers
  • League invites + sharing
  • Draft grades, records, power rankings
  • Manager profiles
↓

V2 — IN-SEASON FEATURES

  • Weekly AI recaps + storylines
  • Live scoring integration
  • Trade analyzer
  • Waiver recommendations
↓

V3 — GROWTH

  • Playoff probability models
  • League awards ceremony
  • Additional sports (basketball, baseball)
  • Premium tier
Don't rush a half-baked product. Build the full vision — all providers, complete analytics, rich AI chat — and launch during the NFL offseason (June-July) when fantasy managers are bored, nostalgic about last season, and starting to think about the upcoming draft. The offseason is when people actually have time to explore historical data and reminisce. A full-featured launch makes a bigger splash, gets better App Store reviews, and takes advantage of the natural fantasy football calendar. V2 adds in-season features (weekly recaps, trade analysis) in time for the NFL season. The risk: this is the longest development timeline, and you're betting you can build everything before the window opens.

Works well when

  • First impression is the complete vision — no compromises
  • Offseason timing aligns perfectly with historical data exploration
  • Better App Store reviews from a polished, feature-complete launch

Watch out for

  • Longest time to market — months of building before any user feedback
  • Risk of over-building features nobody uses
  • If you miss the offseason window, you wait a full year
